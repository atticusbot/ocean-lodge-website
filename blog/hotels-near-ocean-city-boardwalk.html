The Best Hotels Near the Ocean City Boardwalk (And Why Location Really Matters)

By Ocean Lodge Ocean City  ·  Ocean City, MD  ·  March 2026

If you've searched for hotels near the Ocean City boardwalk, you already know how hard it is to read the marketing. "Beachfront access." "Boardwalk adjacent." "Steps from the sand." Everyone says something like it. Almost no one tells you what it actually means in practice.

Here's what to actually look for — and where Ocean Lodge fits in.

Why Location Changes the Whole Trip

Ocean City runs 10 miles north to south. The boardwalk is the bottom three miles — that's where the food, the rides, the evening crowd, and the ocean views are. A hotel at 65th Street isn't the same trip as a hotel at 8th Street, even if both say "Ocean City, MD."

For families especially, walking distance is everything. When the kids want one more boardwalk loop at 8 PM and you're already back at the room, "two blocks" means yes. "Two miles" means no.

What "near the boardwalk" should actually mean:

  • Walking distance in under 10 minutes without driving
  • Close enough to go back to the room mid-day, drop your bags, and return
  • No paying for parking every time you want to get to the sand

What Else to Compare (Beyond Location)

Hotels near the Ocean City boardwalk vary a lot beyond just proximity. A few things worth comparing before you book:

  • Kitchen access. Ocean City dining is expensive in peak season, especially with a family. A kitchenette with a fridge, microwave, and stove lets you do breakfast every morning and a few dinners at the room. On a week-long trip, that's real money saved.
  • Parking. On-site parking that's included — not an upcharge — matters more than it sounds in July. Driving around looking for spots eats into beach time fast.
  • Direct booking. Third-party sites add fees and create a layer between you and the property. Booking direct means a real person to call if anything changes.
  • Who runs the place. Independent, family-owned hotels tend to act differently when something needs attention. There's no corporate escalation process — there's a person making the call.

💡 Quick tip: Always call the hotel directly after finding it online. Ask about direct booking rates — most independent properties offer a discount that doesn't show up on Expedia or Booking.com.
