Ocean City Maryland Family Vacation Guide: Planning, Boardwalk, and Where to Stay

By Ocean Lodge Ocean City  ·  Ocean City, MD  ·  March 2026

An Ocean City Maryland family vacation is one of the classic East Coast summer trips — and for good reason. Wide beaches, a 3-mile boardwalk packed with food and rides, and easy driving distance from a huge chunk of the Mid-Atlantic population. It delivers. But the planning decisions you make before you arrive will shape the whole trip. This guide covers what to do, how to think about where to stay, and why proximity to the boardwalk matters more than most booking sites let on.

What Makes Ocean City a Good Family Destination

Ocean City, Maryland has a few things going for it that make it reliably good for families:

  • The boardwalk. Three miles of boardwalk running from the inlet up to 27th Street. Rides, games, taffy shops, Thrasher's fries, miniature golf, restaurants. For kids, it's endlessly entertaining. For adults, it's a legitimate evening stroll.
  • The beach itself. Wide, sandy, lifeguarded in season. The water is the Atlantic — it's refreshing, not tropical. Kids who want waves will find them; families who want calm can find calmer spots too.
  • Easy logistics. Ocean City is a destination, not a road trip leg. There's one main road, plenty of food options at every budget, and it's compact enough that you don't need a plan every day. Show up, walk around, repeat.

Why Where You Stay Matters for a Family Trip

Ocean City runs 10 miles north to south. The boardwalk is at the southern end. A hotel at 65th Street is technically Ocean City — but it's a 20-minute drive from the boardwalk, which means every boardwalk visit is a production: pack the car, find parking, pay for parking, unpack the car. On a week-long family trip, that friction accumulates.

The calculus for families is different than for couples or solo travelers. With kids, you want:

  • The ability to go back to the room mid-day for naps or a break without losing your parking spot
  • Evening walks to the boardwalk that are actually walkable — not a car trip after dinner
  • One less decision to make about logistics every single day

Two blocks from the boardwalk is meaningfully different from two miles. It's not marketing — it's the difference between a walk and a commute.

📍 Location tip: When searching for hotels in Ocean City, look at the street address, not just the description. Anything below 30th Street puts you in easy walking distance of the boardwalk. Above 50th Street and you're in a different part of the trip entirely.
